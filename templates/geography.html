Peru Website - Geography

Main Geography

 Peru is a country in the Central Western coast of South America. It shares its borders with Ecuador, Colombia, Brazil, Bolivia, and Chile. Peru has a land mass of about 1,380,000 km² (532,821 mi²). Another thing about Peru's geography is that it lies entirely in the Southern Hemisphere. The forests and woodland in peru account for 66% of the landscape. Mountains, beaches, deserts, and rain forests make up the rest of it. Peru is the third largest country in South America, after Brazil and Argentina.

Natural Disasters/Hazards

 Natural disasters that can occur in Peru include earthquakes, tsunamis, flooding, landslides and, sometimes, volcanic activity.

Natural Resources

 Peru has some of the most natural resources out of all the countries in the world. Some of its natural resources include:

  • Gold
  • Silver
  • Copper
  • Zinc
  • Lead
  • Iron
  • Oil & Natural Gas

 Although Peru has so much resources, jobs there can be very hard to find and Peru, sadly, still remains as one of the world's poorest countries.
